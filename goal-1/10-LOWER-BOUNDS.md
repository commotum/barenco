## Current Facts

- The only proved lower-bound argument in the paper's Sections 3–7 is Lemma 7.7.
  It concerns exact simulation of a fully controlled nonscalar one-qubit unitary
  by arbitrary one-qubit gates and CNOTs. Although the statement says at least
  `n-1` "basic operations," the proof actually establishes the stronger claim
  that at least `n-1` CNOT occurrences are necessary; arbitrary one-qubit gates
  do not add edges to the interaction graph.
- The implemented invariant is `BasicCircuit.interactionGraph`, the undirected
  graph on the register wires generated by CNOT occurrences. One-qubit operations
  add no edges. Repeated CNOTs on one wire pair remain distinct occurrences but
  collapse to one simple-graph edge, so
  `interactionGraph_edgeFinset_card_le_cnotCount` is an inequality, not an
  equality. Connectedness therefore implies `n-1 <= cnotCount`.
- The semantic step suppressed by the source is substantial and is now explicit.
  `wireSplit` groups any `Finset (Fin n)` of wires first, including noncontiguous
  cuts; `partitionReindex` fixes the corresponding row/column convention; and
  `TensorFactorsAcross` asks for a raw Kronecker factorization in that order.
  Local one-qubit gates and same-side CNOTs factor, factorization is closed under
  chronological circuit multiplication, and
  `eval_tensorFactorsAcross_targetComponent` packages the disconnected case.
  Orienting the cut as the component containing the target then loses no case.
- `isScalarQubitMatrix_of_tensorFactorsAcross_positiveControlledRaw` proves the
  missing obstruction: if a listed positive control is separated from the target,
  factorization forces the target matrix to be scalar. Its fully controlled
  specialization gives nonfactorization for every proper target-containing cut.
- `Circuit` permits several macro and unclassified primitive kinds. The new
  proof-carrying `BasicPrimitive` has exactly `.oneQubit` and distinct-wire `.cnot`
  constructors, and `BasicCircuit` is a chronological list of them. Erasure uses
  the trusted generic smart constructors and has exact evaluator, occurrence-count,
  gate-count, and `CostModel.oneQubitCNOT` bridges. The lower bound therefore
  quantifies over a semantically classified primitive set rather than inferring
  locality from `Primitive.kind` metadata.
- The nonscalar boundary is essential. `IsScalarQubitMatrix` is reconciled with
  the paper's certified form by
  `isScalarQubitMatrix_coe_iff_exists_phaseShiftUnitary`. If
  `U = Ph(delta)`, its fully controlled version is a control-dependent phase and
  lacks the separated target dependence used by the obstruction. The exported
  lower bound excludes exactly this case. At one register wire `n-1=0`; the graph
  theorem handles that trivial boundary without fabricating a proper cut. A
  zero-wire instance cannot supply the required `target : Fin n`.
- `fullyControlled_cnotCount_lowerBound` is the headline exact theorem. The
  paper's weaker total-basic-operation claim is
  `fullyControlled_gateCount_lowerBound`, and
  `fullyControlled_oneQubitCNOTCost_lowerBound` links the same result to a numeric
  accepted cost of the erased syntax. All three are exact, same-register,
  no-ancilla statements; they do not cover approximation, phase-relaxed equality,
  measurement, or ancilla-assisted implementations.
- Section 5 gives topology classifications for two-CNOT and one-CNOT circuit
  families, already formalized as `twoCNOTFamily_iff` and `oneCNOTFamily_iff`.
  These are exact iff results for named topologies, not global minimum-CNOT
  theorems over every possible circuit.
- Corollaries 5.3 and 6.2 are constructive "at most" results. Section 8 explicitly
  says its small arbitrary-two-qubit counts were supported by numerical evidence,
  not proved minimal. No minimum theorem may be inferred from those upper bounds.
- Section 8's six-gate `U(8)` diagram reaches the dimension `64`, but equality of
  parameter dimensions does not prove surjectivity. Its formula
  `(4^n-3n-1)/9` is presented as a dimension-counting suggestion/conjecture and
  lacks a formal parameter map, smooth image/fiber argument, quotient treatment,
  and generic-versus-worst-case quantifier. It remains excluded unless those
  missing mathematical ingredients are supplied independently.

## Boundary and No-Cheating Checks

- The allowed primitive set is part of every headline theorem; Toffoli,
  controlled-one-qubit macros, arbitrary-two-qubit primitives, and unclassified
  semantic gates are not admitted accidentally.
- Edge cardinality is bounded by CNOT occurrences, not identified with them;
  repeated gates on the same pair may produce duplicate graph edges.
- Disconnection produces a named nonempty proper partition and an exact semantic
  factorization, not merely a statement about syntactic supports.
- The Kronecker order follows the chosen basis equivalence and is verified by
  entries/evaluation; paper notation does not choose the Lean index order.
- "Nonscalar" is defined algebraically and reconciled with the paper's unit-phase
  scalar form for certified unitaries.
- Exact equality, global-phase equality, channel equality, and basis behavior are
  not interchanged. The first lower-bound theorem targets exact circuit equality.
- A structural lower bound is not generalized to ancilla-assisted, measurement,
  approximate, or arbitrary-two-qubit models without a new invariant.
- No result is called minimal or `Omega` merely because a named construction has
  a matching-looking count.
- No `sorry`, `admit`, `by?`, custom `axiom`, `opaque`, `native_decide`, or
  `bv_decide` may occur in completed modules.
